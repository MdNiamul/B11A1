Launched in May 2018, Bangabandhu-1 is Bangladesh's first geostationary communications satellite. Developed by Thales Alenia Space, it aimed to provide services such as direct-to-home (DTH) broadcasting, VSAT communications, backhaul connectivity, and disaster preparedness. Despite its ambitious goals, the satellite has faced financial challenges, with reports indicating annual losses and concerns about the recovery of the initial investment.

In August 2024, Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina's government was ousted following mass protests, particularly student-led demonstrations opposing the reinstated quota system for public sector jobs. These protests resulted in over 300 fatalities. The interim government, led by Nobel laureate Muhammad Yunus, is tasked with restoring democratic processes and addressing human rights concerns.
